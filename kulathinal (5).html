Who We Are

A Family Rooted in Rubber Farming

We are the Kulathinal family — a second-generation rubber estate management enterprise proudly rooted in the fertile lands of Dakshina Kannada. Since the 1960s, our family has been walking rubber estates, understanding the soil, reading the seasons, and delivering consistent yields for plantation owners across the region.

We don't just manage estates — we treat each plantation as our own. From careful fertilisation to structured tapping schedules, every step we take is grounded in decades of experience and deep respect for the land.

Annual Schedule

Monthly Plantation Care Plan

Our systematic monthly care calendar is tailored to the specific climatic conditions of the Mangalore–Dakshina Kannada region to ensure high yield and long-term plantation health.

January 🌤 Dry
  • Tapping continues as usual
  • Inspect for pest attacks; initiate early control
  • General upkeep of tapping tools
February 🌤 Dry
  • Begin preparing for summer break
  • First round of weed removal
  • Check tree health due to rising temperatures
March ☀ Pre-summer
  • Apply fertiliser – First Dose
  • Continue tapping
  • Begin rain guard application (2nd half)
  • Plan for tapping break next month
April 🔆 Break
  • Summer Break – No Tapping
  • Continue rain guard application
  • General maintenance of paths & gutters
  • Weed control if required
May 🔆 Break
  • Summer break continues
  • Mulching around plants to preserve moisture
  • Clean rain guards and tapping panels
June 🌧 Monsoon
  • Monsoon begins – resume tapping (2nd half)
  • Monitor waterlogging
  • Strengthen drainage channels
July 🌧 Monsoon
  • Tapping continues with rain guards
  • Prune overgrown branches
  • Pest monitoring – fungal issues
August 🌧 Monsoon
  • Mid-season tapping
  • Maintain rain guards
  • Second round of weed removal
September 🌦 Receding
  • Fertility check of soil if needed
  • Mid-year inspection of tree health
  • Repair damaged panels or guards
October 🌤 Post-monsoon
  • Apply fertiliser – Second Dose
  • Reassess tapping panels
  • Prepare for high-yield season
November 🌤 Peak
  • Tapping in full swing
  • Systematic latex collection & processing
  • Check for fungal infections
December 🌤 Peak
  • Continue tapping
  • Annual review & planning for next year
  • Recheck fencing & plantation infrastructure
